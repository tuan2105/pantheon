The Exhibition Tournament

The Rules of Combat

  • Combat Wheel: There are 6 combat styles. Every style naturally defeats 3 other styles, and loses to 2.
  • Advantage (Left vs Right): In any matchup, the fighter with the favored combat style takes the Left Spot. The disadvantaged fighter starts in the ring, taking the Right Spot.
  • The Ring Advantage: The ring (Right Spot) grants a slight tactical advantage. However, Combat Style advantage is stronger. Therefore, normally, the Favored fighter (Left Spot) wins.
  • Unexpected Results: If the fighter with the disadvantaged style (in the Ring/Right Spot) manages to win anyway, this is classed as an "Unexpected Result".
  • Same Style Matchups: If two fighters use the exact same combat style, there is no style advantage. The fighter originating from the higher-numbered starting spot (e.g., Spot 2 over Spot 1, or the 5-8 bracket over the 1-4 bracket) takes the Right Spot. Because they share a style, the Ring Advantage decides the match: the fighter in the Right Spot will always win.
